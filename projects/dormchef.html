DormChef

Social meal-planning app helping college students cook affordable, healthy meals in tiny kitchens by sharing and organizing recipes with friends.

The Challenge

College students want to eat healthy and save money, but cooking in dorm kitchens is intimidating, expensive, and isolating. Most meal-planning apps are built for families with full kitchens, not 19-year-olds with a hot plate and no cooking experience.

After interviewing 40+ college students, I discovered that the biggest barriers weren't just about recipes or budgeting ‚Äî they were about confidence, community, and equipment constraints.

Key Insights from User Research

  • 87% of students said they wanted to cook more but felt overwhelmed
  • 73% of students wished they could cook with friends more often
  • 91% of students had limited kitchen equipment (hot plate, mini fridge, microwave)
  • 65% of students said existing meal apps were "too complicated" or "not for college"

The Solution

DormChef is a social meal-planning app designed specifically for college students living in dorms. It combines recipe discovery, meal planning, and social cooking to make healthy eating accessible, affordable, and fun.

Design Process

1. User Research & Discovery

I spent 3 weeks conducting interviews with college students across 5 universities. I visited dorm kitchens, observed cooking sessions, and analyzed existing meal-planning apps to understand the unique constraints of the college market.

2. Ideation & Concept Development

Based on research insights, I developed the concept of "social cooking for students." The key insight was that cooking is more enjoyable and less intimidating when done with friends, but existing apps treated meal planning as an individual activity.

3. Wireframing & Prototyping

I created low-fidelity wireframes focusing on three core user journeys: discovering recipes, planning meals with friends, and tracking budgets. The interface needed to be simple enough for busy students but powerful enough to handle group coordination.

4. Visual Design & Branding

The visual design needed to feel approachable and energetic ‚Äî more like a social app than a productivity tool. I chose a warm color palette inspired by fresh ingredients and created playful illustrations for cooking techniques.

Key Learnings

1. Social Features Drive Engagement

The most successful users were those who invited friends to the app. Cooking together transformed meal planning from a chore into a social activity, leading to 3x higher retention rates.

2. Equipment Constraints Are Creative Constraints

Limiting recipes to basic dorm equipment wasn't a limitation ‚Äî it was a feature. Users appreciated the clarity and felt confident they could actually make the recipes.

3. Students Want Education, Not Just Recipes

The cooking tutorial features had unexpectedly high engagement. Many students lacked basic cooking knowledge, and providing education alongside recipes built confidence and loyalty.

4. Mobile-First is Essential

Students primarily used the app while grocery shopping or in their dorm kitchens. The mobile experience needed to be flawless, with offline capabilities for recipes.

What's Next

Based on user feedback and usage data, I'm focusing on three key areas for DormChef's next iteration:

  • Campus Integration: Partnerships with university dining services and local grocery stores for special student pricing
  • AI Meal Suggestions: Smart recommendations based on dietary preferences, budget, and available ingredients
  • Community Features: Recipe sharing, cooking challenges, and campus cooking events
